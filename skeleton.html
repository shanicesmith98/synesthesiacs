What is Synthesia?

Occurring in less than 4% of the population, synesthesia (pronounced si·nuh·stee·zhuh) is a fascinating condition in which the stimulation of one sense activates another sense. Some people with synesthesia can taste an image. Others can feel the texture of a sound. The combinations are seemingly endless! It’s important to remember that synesthesia between senses is only one sided. Someone who can feel the texture of a taste wouldn't be able to taste the texture of an object. The name, when broken down, is pretty self explanatory: syn, which means "together", and aesthesia, which means "sensation". Someone who has synesthesia is called a synesthete. There’s one certain combination that is well recurring in synesthetes: the ability to see sound. It’s called chromesthesia, when a sound can conjure up colors in a person’s mind.
